#  Threat, Vulnerability, Risk and Attack
# Threat(a possibility) âš ï¸ 
-  Threats are **POSSIBLE** **events, sources, actions, or inactions**(not doing something) that could **potentially lead to harm of your organizations information security assets.
- Once a threat becomes active, it is considered an attack.
- A threat is a **combination of Threat actions and Threat sources**.
- Threat = Threat Sources + Threat Actions
- **A Threat source** can have **more than one threat action.**
- Ex:
	- Threat source: Hacker
	- Threat action: Unauthorized access
	- Threat: **(Unauthorized access)** by **(Hacker)**

### Disclosure Threat 
- Dissemination of Unauthorized Information
- Compromises the **Confidentiality** aspect of CIA.
### Integrity Threat 
- Unauthorized modification of Data.
- Compromises the **Integrity** aspect of CIA.
### Denial of Service Threat 
- Access to a system or service is limited or blocked for authorized individuals.
- Compromises the **Availability** aspect of CIA.

- Most vulnerabilities are **exploited by automated processes** rather **than with direct human activity.**
- Ex:
	-Weak password, Unencrypted Data

# Components  of Risk
- Event 
- Asset
- Outcome
- Probability
## Event ğŸ“…
- Always a **future** event.
- It can be an **action** or **not taking an action**.
- The **action/inaction** will have a **direct/indirect influence** on the **outcome**.
- Ex: 
	Using a weak password(action)
	Not using secure methods for data transfer(inaction)
## Asset ğŸ’
- An asset is **anything valuable** within the organization.
- It is the direct or indirect **target of an event.**
- The outcome always have a **direct consequence applied to the asset.**
- Ex: Applications, Databases, Software, Hardware, People
## OutcomeğŸ’¥
- This is the **negative impact** of the event.
- The outcome of a risk is **always unfavorable**.
- This always has a direct effect on part of / the whole asset.
- Ex:
	**$150,000 in recovery costs** (e.g., forensics, system replacement) and **$50,000 in lost revenue** due to downtime. 
## Probability %
- This is the measurement / probability of an event occurring in the future.
- Ex: 
	**Expected Frequency: 2-3 successful interceptions per year** by external actors due to known network vulnerabilities.
